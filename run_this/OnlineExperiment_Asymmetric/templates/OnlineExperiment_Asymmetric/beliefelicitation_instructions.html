Explaining the Guess Task:

Description of the Task

For the Guess task, we ask you to provide your best guess of how much effort your worker will provide at each possible wage level.

While you complete this task, the worker will be completing a similar one, providing the actual amount of effort for each wage level.

Your objective is to guess his/her effort responses. Your payoff for the Guess Task is based on the accuracy of your guesses.

Accuracy of Guesses

We consider your guess as ‘accurate’ if it is within .25 of the actual effort level the worker agrees to provide for a given wage. For example, if your guess is 0.5 and the actual value is 0.25, 0.5 or 0.75, then your guess is considered accurate.

How to earn money with the Guess Task:

We will pay you based on the accuracy of your guesses.
The computer will randomly select one of your guesses (namely, one row of the table above) and if the guess is 'accurate', then you will win $5.
If your randomly chosen guess is not 'accurate', then you earn nothing.

An example: Suppose the computer chooses to evaluate your guess for a wage = $6, namely the row 6 of the table above. Assume that your guess here is that your worker will provide effort = 1.25. If the real amount of effort provided by the worker is an effort of 1, 1.25, or 1.5 then you will earn $5. Otherwise, you will earn nothing.
