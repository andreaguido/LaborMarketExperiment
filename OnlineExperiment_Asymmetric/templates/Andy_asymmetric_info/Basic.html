Basic Information and Roles

We will randomly designate you to be either an employer or a worker (these roles are described below) and then randomly pair one employer with one worker. Your role never changes during the experiment and you remain with the same worker/employer for the entire experiment. The experiment lasts for a total of 20 decision periods. No other participant will know your identity or the actions you take during this experiment.

  • EMPLOYER: A subject assigned to the role of 'employer' will receive an endowment of $10 at the beginning of each period. All employers receive the same endowment. An employer will use this endowment to pay a wage to his/her worker that period. Once the employer sets this wage, that employer's assigned worker views the wage and then selects a corresponding level of costly effort. Whatever portion of this endowment the employer keeps is then multiplied by the worker's effort and paid to the employer. Thus, the higher the level of effort selected by the worker, the more money the employer earns.
    An employer can pay his/her worker a wage from 1 to 10 points. That is, an employer can select w=[1,2,3,...,10]. Once an employer selects a wage, the worker will view this wage and select a costly level of effort in response. Based on the wage the employer selects and the level of costly effort the worker selects, the employer will earn:
    Employer's payoff = (endowment - wage)* effort level

    At the end of each period, the employer will learn the worker's effort level and his/her own payoff. Note that a worker selects a new wage in each period.

  • WORKERS: A subject assigned to the role of 'worker' will receive a wage offer in each period from his/her corresponding employer. After viewing the employer's wage offer, the worker will select a level of costly effort for that period. Workers can select effort, e, so that e = [0, 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, ..., 4]. Workers will know the cost of each potential effort level, the earnings of the employer, and his/her own earnings for each potential effort level. After a worker selects an effort level, both the employer and the worker learn their payoffs for that period. Note that the worker selects a new level of costly effort in every period. Workers have the following payoff function:
    Worker's payoff = wage - cost of effort

  • A round will proceed as follows:

    • An employer receives an endowment and then sets a wage for the worker.
    • The worker learns the wage offered and then selects a level of costly effort.
    • Finally, employers observe the effort level and both subjects learn their payoff for that period.
